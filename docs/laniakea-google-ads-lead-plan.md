# Laniakea Beach House Google Ads Lead Plan

Prepared for Justin and Manoela

## Executive Summary

Google Search ads are worth testing for Laniakea Beach House, but only as a tightly scoped, compliance-first lead funnel for legal extended stays. The opportunity exists because many North Shore Oahu vacation-rental searches still have high intent, while Airbnb and Vrbo inventory may be constrained by local rental rules. As owners, the economics can work: one good extended-stay booking can justify a few thousand dollars of testing.

The plan should not be framed as a way to avoid enforcement. It should be framed as a direct-owner inquiry channel for people who want a legitimate furnished oceanfront stay on the North Shore and can comply with the legal minimum rental period.

Recommended first move:

1. Confirm the legal minimum stay and required advertisement wording before launch.
2. Update the website to position the home as a compliant extended-stay beach house.
3. Launch a small Google Search-only campaign with exact and phrase-match keywords.
4. Route inquiries to Manoela or a shared inquiry inbox.
5. Use a consistent fraud-prevention and identity-verification process for all serious leads.

Expected owner time:

- Justin: 6-10 hours for setup and review, then 30-60 minutes per week.
- Manoela: 2-4 hours for setup and tone review, then 15-45 minutes per week unless lead volume is high.
- Codex/AI: Can handle most drafting, website edits, keyword lists, ad copy, inquiry templates, tracker structure, and weekly campaign analysis.

## Bottom-Line Recommendation

Run a small test. Do not overbuild.

The first version should answer one question:

> Can Google Search produce qualified extended-stay inquiries at a cost that makes sense for an owner-operated luxury beach house?

The first test should run for 2-4 weeks with a controlled budget of about $1,500-$3,000. If the campaign produces even one serious qualified lead, keep refining. If it only produces short-stay bargain hunters, pause and rework the positioning.

## The Big Compliance Caveat

Before ads go live, confirm the required minimum stay and advertisement language. This is the biggest risk in the plan.

Current research shows a confusing surface:

- Honolulu DPP's short-term rental page says the city continues to enforce STRs as rentals of less than 30 consecutive days.
- The DPP FAQ says ads for non-registered/non-NUC rentals must include required language about not renting for less than the applicable consecutive-day minimum.
- The April 2026 Land Use Ordinance text includes 90-day language for unregistered/non-NUC dwelling units and says ads may not include daily or less-than-three-month rental rates.

This means the website and ads should not casually say "30-day minimum" unless that has been confirmed for this property. If 90 days applies, the page and ads need to reflect that.

Practical rule:

> No ad, page, email, or inquiry response should offer nightly, weekly, or partial-period pricing.

Recommended compliance wording, pending legal confirmation:

> Laniakea Beach House is available only for legal extended stays. Minimum rental period and required disclosures will be confirmed before booking. No nightly or weekly rentals are offered.

If counsel or DPP confirms that a specific statement is required, use that exact statement on the landing page and anywhere pricing or availability appears.

## Why Google Search Is The Best First Channel

Google Search is a better first channel than social media because the intent is much clearer.

Someone searching for "north shore oahu beach house rental" or "oahu oceanfront monthly rental" is already looking for a place to stay. On social media, we would need to interrupt people and then qualify them, and housing-related ads can trigger special ad category restrictions. Google also has housing-related targeting restrictions, but Search lets us rely more on keywords and page content instead of demographic targeting.

Use Search first. Avoid Display, YouTube, Performance Max, and broad social ads until there is proof that the landing page and inquiry process convert.

Codex and AI can remove most of the busywork.

AI can do effectively:

- Rewrite the website and landing page for compliant extended-stay positioning.
- Draft page titles, meta descriptions, headings, FAQs, and inquiry CTAs.
- Produce Google Ads campaign structure.
- Draft ad headlines, descriptions, sitelinks, callouts, and structured snippets.
- Build keyword and negative-keyword lists.
- Create a lead qualification form and scoring rubric.
- Create email templates for first response, follow-up, and rejection.
- Draft a weekly campaign review checklist.
- Analyze exported Google Ads search-term reports and recommend changes.
- Produce a simple lead tracker in Markdown, CSV, Google Sheets format, or a lightweight local file.

AI should not own:

- Legal conclusions about the minimum stay.
- Final public wording approval.
- Google Ads billing and account ownership.
- Whether to accept a particular guest.
- Identity verification judgment calls.
- Signing rental agreements or collecting deposits.

## Phase 1: Compliance And Positioning

Goal: Decide what the website and ads are allowed to say.

Actions:

1. Confirm whether the property must advertise a 30-day or 90-day minimum.
2. Confirm whether the exact DPP-required statement is needed on the page.
3. Confirm whether the property has any NUC, STR registration, zoning, or prior-use facts that affect minimum-stay treatment.
4. Confirm tax handling for stays under 180 days, including Hawaii TAT and Oahu TAT if applicable.
5. Confirm whether Airbnb/Vrbo listings should remain linked, hidden, or reframed.

Output:

- A one-paragraph approved compliance statement.
- A confirmed minimum stay.
- A decision on whether the landing page can mention "vacation rental" or should use "extended stay", "monthly rental", "furnished beach house", or similar wording.

Recommended public positioning:

> Oceanfront furnished beach house on Oahu's North Shore for compliant extended stays.

Avoid:

- "Nightly rental"
- "Weekly rental"
- "Book a few nights"
- "Pay for 30 days but only stay 10"
- Daily prices
- Calendar language that implies shorter stays are available

## Phase 2: Email And Inquiry Routing

Goal: Make it easy for serious people to contact Manoela or the owners directly.

Recommended setup:

1. Add `LaniakeaBeachHouse.com` to Google Workspace as a secondary domain if you want only selected addresses on that domain.
2. Create `manoela@laniakeabeachhouse.com` as either a real user or an alias, depending on whether Manoela wants a separate mailbox.
3. Create `inquiries@laniakeabeachhouse.com` or `info@laniakeabeachhouse.com` as a Google Group or alias.
4. Route form submissions to the group.
5. Add Gmail labels:
   - `Laniakea/New Inquiry`
   - `Laniakea/Qualified`
   - `Laniakea/Needs Reply`
   - `Laniakea/Not A Fit`
6. Add canned replies for common scenarios.

Secondary domain vs user alias domain:

- Use a secondary domain if Laniakea Beach House should be controlled as its own mini-brand with selected users and aliases.
- Use a user alias domain only if every existing Workspace user should automatically get an address at `LaniakeaBeachHouse.com`.

Recommendation: secondary domain.

## Phase 3: Landing Page

Recommended page:

1. Hero:

   - Is this available for nightly stays?

7. Trust:
   - Direct owner inquiry.
   - Existing Airbnb/Vrbo presence if appropriate.
   - Prior guest reviews, if legally and platform-compliantly reusable.

Important:

If the law requires a specific statement about not reducing or adjusting rental prices based on actual occupancy, include it exactly.

## Phase 4: Google Ads Test Campaign

Goal: Buy high-intent search traffic, not broad awareness.

Campaign type:

- Google Search only.
- No Display Network.
- No Performance Max at launch.
- No broad match at launch.
- Use phrase and exact match.
- Manual CPC or maximize clicks with a strict CPC cap at first.

Initial budget:

- Conservative: $50/day for 21 days, about $1,050.
- Recommended: $100/day for 21 days, about $2,100.
- Aggressive test: $150/day for 21 days, about $3,150.

Recommended first test: $100/day for 21 days.

## Phase 5: Lead Qualification

Goal: Consistently separate real prospects from poor-fit inquiries without creating fair-housing or platform-policy issues.

Recommended lead stages:

1. New inquiry
2. Minimum-stay fit
3. Date and budget fit
4. Identity and fraud-prevention review
5. Agreement/payment review
6. Booked or declined

Use consistent questions for everyone:

- Desired dates
- Intended stay duration
- Number of guests
- Whether they understand the minimum-stay requirement
- Whether they are comfortable with identity verification
- Best phone/email
- Optional LinkedIn or social profile
- Optional reason for extended stay

Avoid questions that could create fair-housing problems:

- Do not ask about marital status.
- Do not ask about family status beyond neutral occupancy/guest-count needs.
- Do not ask about protected classes.
- Do not imply a preference for or against families, children, age groups, national origin, religion, disability, etc.

Identity verification should be described as fraud prevention, not enforcement avoidance.

Recommended language:

> For safety and fraud prevention, we verify identity for all direct inquiries before confirming a booking. We may ask for a government ID, a brief video call, a signed agreement, and a verified payment method.

Avoid:

> We need to make sure you are not an enforcement agent.

## Phase 6: Booking Workflow

Goal: Make the path from inquiry to booking clear and documented.

Proposed flow:

1. Lead submits inquiry.
2. Manoela or owner sends initial response.
3. Confirm minimum stay, dates, guest count, and fit.
4. Send rate quote for the full legal rental period.
5. Conduct identity/fraud-prevention review.
6. Send rental agreement and payment instructions.
7. Confirm booking only after signed agreement and required payment.
8. Block calendar across all channels.
9. Save all records in one folder or tracker.

If Airbnb is involved:

- Do not use Airbnb messaging to steer guests off Airbnb.
- If someone starts on Airbnb, keep Airbnb-related booking activity on Airbnb unless platform rules clearly allow otherwise.
- Direct-booking leads should come through the direct website, not through an Airbnb conversation that is moved off-platform.

## Economics And Success Model

The owner economics can justify paid search.

Example:

- Ad spend: $3,000.
- Average CPC: $4.
- Clicks: 750.
- Landing-page inquiry rate: 3%.
- Raw inquiries: 22.
- Qualified rate: 20-30%.
- Qualified leads: 4-7.
- Bookings needed to justify test: 1.

If one booking fills otherwise empty dates and produces $8,000+ of incremental value, then even a $3,000-$4,000 acquisition cost can make sense.

The key is not cost per click. The key is cost per qualified lead and cost per booked stay.

## Metrics To Track

Weekly:

- Spend
- Clicks
- Average CPC
- Click-through rate
- Form submissions
- Cost per inquiry
- Qualified inquiries
- Cost per qualified inquiry
- Booked stays
- Booked revenue
- Search terms that wasted money
- Search terms that look promising

Decision thresholds:

- Pause keywords with high spend and no inquiries.
- Add negatives for short-stay, bargain, hotel, apartment, job, and unrelated searches.
- Rewrite ads if click-through rate is low.
- Rewrite landing-page above-the-fold copy if clicks do not become inquiries.
- Stop the first test if $1,500-$2,000 produces no serious inquiries.
- Scale only after at least one qualified lead appears.
